# Reference Corpus: Awwwards Winners

Study these. Not to copy — to understand why they won.

---

## Site of the Year Winners

### Igloo Inc (SOTY 2024)
**Studio:** abeto  
**Vibes:** minimal + luxury  
**Cluster:** luxury-premium

**Why It Won:**
Seamless shared-element transitions that feel impossible. Elements morph between pages without any sense of "loading." The restraint is extreme — near-monochrome, minimal motion — which makes the transitions feel like magic.

**What to Learn:**
- Transitions between states > animations on single states
- Monochrome palettes amplify any color moment
- "Seamless" is the goal, not "impressive"

---

### Lusion v3 (SOTY 2023)
**Studio:** Lusion  
**Vibes:** experimental + futuristic  
**Cluster:** experimental-art

**Why It Won:**
WebGL fluid distortion that follows the cursor. The portfolio pieces are secondary to the experience of navigating. Every hover is a discovery.

**What to Learn:**
- Cursor-led motion creates exploration
- Technical craft CAN be the concept (for portfolios)
- Consistency — the distortion is EVERYWHERE

---

### Bruno Simon Portfolio (SOTY 2019)
**Studio:** Bruno Simon  
**Vibes:** experimental + playful  
**Cluster:** experimental-art

**Why It Won:**
You drive a 3D car around a miniature world to explore the portfolio. The metaphor is perfect: "explore my work" becomes literal exploration.

**What to Learn:**
- Metaphor made interactive = signature moment
- Gamification works if it's the point
- One wild idea > ten safe ones

## How to Use This Corpus

1. **Match your brief to similar winners** — Find 2-3 sites with overlapping vibes/industry
2. **Study what they did, not how** — Techniques can transfer, but concepts must be original
3. **Identify the ONE thing** — Each winner has one signature element. What's yours?
4. **Note the restraint** — What did they NOT do? That's often more instructive.
